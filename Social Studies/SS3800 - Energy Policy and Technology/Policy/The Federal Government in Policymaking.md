## Legislative Branch
- Has the leading role in making [[Policy]]
- Controls money and legislation

## Executive Branch
### POTUS
- Not supposed to be making policy 
- Can pressure & suggest & work with congress
- Main role is outlining the policy agenda/big picture. 
- Relies on the legislative branch and administrative agencies to actually make policy. 
- Organizes things
- Hires people
### Administrative Agencies
- Does the prep work for Congress
- Involved in Administrative Rulemaking
	- Set based on an existing legislation & precedent
	- Thoughtful & structured process.
- Control licensing & permitting.


## Judicial Branch
- both federal and state branches
- 94 Federal Judicial districts made up of 12 circuits.
- Not usually considered to form public policy, but they create legal precedent which creates the power for change.
- Control [[Judicial Standing]]
