## Autonomic Nerve Pathways

^93d903

- Unlike with somatic motor neurons that use a single axon extending to the effector, autonomic motor neurons usually are made up of **two motor neurons in series**. 
	- The first [[Myelination of Neurons|Myelinated]] [[Preganglionic Neurons]] extends from the [[Central Nervous System]] to the Autonomic Ganglion (a collection of neuronal cells). The second **unmyelinated** neuron extends from the ganglion to the effector.
	- Alternatively, some of the first neurons interact with Chromaffin cells in the adrenal medullae (inside adrenal glands), which produce **epinephrine and norepinephrine**
		- Autonomic Motor Neurons can release ACH or Norepinephrine
