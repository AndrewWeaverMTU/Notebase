### Formula
$$\vec{E} = \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0}\frac{q}{r^2}\hat{r}$$
- $\hat{r}$ is a unit vector that points away from $q$ 
- $\epsilon_0 = 8.85 \times 10^-12 \frac{C^2}{N*m^2}$

## Multiple Point Charges
- Electric fields obey superposition - If they stack on top of each other, they add
- $\vec{E}_\text{net} = \frac{\vec{F}_{\text{on }q}}{q} = \frac{\vec{F}_{\text{1 on }q}}{q} + \frac{\vec{F}_{\text{2 on }q}}{q} + \dots = \sum_i \frac{\vec{F}_{i\text{ on }q}}{q} = \sum_i \vec{E}_i$
- just do vector addition lol
